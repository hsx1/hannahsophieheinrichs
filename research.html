Research

I am mostly interested in these areas:

Interests

  • Statistical modeling — Modeling hierarchical data, e.g. longitudinal studies and statistical inference. Statistical inference, incl. frequentist and bayesian inference.
  • Open Science & Research Practices — Formulating a precise research question and sticking to the selected methods is a crucial aspect of statistical inference, especially when using frequentist statistics. As valid inference is tied to a priori information and the universe of statistical approaches/pipelines utilized. Preregistration is a great way thoroughly plan analysis steps based on simulated data without accessing the data.
  • Neuroimaging methods — Application of different methods for analyzing DTI, MRI and other neuroimaging data, as well as the selection and implementation of statistical models for high-dimensional data, such as hierarchical models.
  • Neurobiology — How do immunological and matabolic factors that impact the functional and structural connectivity and the brian microstructure. I am further involved in a deep learning project for the prediction of brain aging, that may be extended to other variables using transfer learning.
  • Cognitive models — I would like to dive more into cognitive research in the future, which I started to look into during my bachelor theses and master studies. Specifically, probabilistic models for decision-making, as well as computational models of memory and the organization of knowledge representations. I am further looking to learn about machine learning methods to inform and optimize human cognitive processes.
